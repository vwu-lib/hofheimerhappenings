## Use Our Services

### Library Research Instruction & Student Research Consultations

Librarians are available and ready to provide library research instruction for classes at all levels. Faculty are encouraged to incorporate and schedule research instruction, which can be requested using the form available [here](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfryYKhRkL64flJEYGKLFDlihzDnSW3VkDqDeJE6geLdwLUrg/viewform).

Research instruction has proven to be an important factor in student success. Students are also welcome and encouraged to schedule individual research consultations with a librarian; more information can be found [here](https://guides.vwu.edu/help).

## Displays

### Black Joy & Resistance

This year’s **Black History Month** display focuses on themes of joy and resistance, both in the past and in our current historical context. Stop by the library to check out titles such as [*The Black Joy Project*](https://vwu.on.worldcat.org/search/detail/1376495515?databaseList=&queryString=the%20black%20joy%20project) (2023) by Kleaver Cruz and [*We Refuse: A Forceful History of Black Resistance*](https://vwu.on.worldcat.org/search/detail/1401907096?queryString=ti%3Awe%20refuse&databaseList=&clusterResults=true&groupVariantRecords=false&bookReviews=off) (2024) by Kellie Carter Jackson.

### Classics Display

Looking to have your mind blown by literary classics and classic films? Check out the library’s new **Classics Display** to get your hands on some of the most renowned books and films we have to offer. Titles include [*Cane*](https://vwu.on.worldcat.org/search/detail/1031918468?queryString=ti%3Acane&bookReviews=off&databaseList=&clusterResults=true&groupVariantRecords=false) (2019 edition) by Jean Toomer and [*The Matrix*](https://vwu.on.worldcat.org/search/detail/162505700?queryString=the%20matrix&bookReviews=off&databaseList=&clusterResults=true&groupVariantRecords=false#availability-section) (1999), directed by Lilly and Lana Wachowski.

## Collections

### Lego Room Now Open!

The library is happy to announce its new **Lego Room**! Located in the Mary Morton Parsons Foundation Student Lounge (which previously housed the library’s 3D printer), the Lego Room serves as a space where students, faculty, and staff can come together to build and destress.

The Lego Room is open **Monday through Friday, from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM**, with plans to expand its hours later in the semester. Check out the library’s Instagram for updates on the Lego Room and other exciting events.

**Please note:** Legos **must stay inside the Lego Room** and may not be checked out.

Have Legos you’d like to donate? Contact our Outreach & Access Services Librarian, **Kelsi Dunman**, at [kdunman@vwu.edu](mailto:kdunman@vwu.edu) for more information, or drop off donations at the Circulation Desk!

## Electronic Resources

### Library Research Award

Updated information on the annual **Library Research Award** is now available. This **$125 cash award** is presented annually at the Spring Honors Convocation to recognize outstanding use of library resources with a project completed during the **2024–25 academic year**.

The deadline for submissions is **Wednesday, April 23**. More information about the award and the application process can be found [here](https://guides.vwu.edu/help).
